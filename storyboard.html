Positioning & audience

Core positioning

If M365 Copilot is your everyday AI assistant, CLI is your operational Copilot — for repeatable, tool-connected, inspectable, longer-running work across the partner organization.

The Copilot Ladder

  • M365 Copilot — helps you with work (draft, summarize, find)
  • GitHub Copilot CLI — operationalizes work (multi-step, multi-source, repeatable)
  • Agency — Microsoft integration layer (internal auth, shared plugins, telemetry, governance)
  • Squad — multi-agent coordination (long-running, role-divided, memory-heavy)

Audience

  • Who: Partner-facing and partner-enablement roles — PSA, PTS, PDM, Partner GTM, PSS, partner solution leads, managers, community leads. Broad across partner-facing functions, not just one sub-role.
  • Level: Non-coders or light-technical users. Many already use M365 Copilot daily.
  • Size: 5–15 people. Hybrid delivery (Teams + in-room).
What attendees walk away with
  • A working Copilot CLI connected to PMX, MSX, and M365
  • Two tested workflow patterns: partner briefing factory + workshop-to-action-plan
  • A cheat sheet with reusable prompts and key commands
  • An understanding of the Copilot Ladder (M365 → CLI → Agency → Squad)
  • Links to the self-paced beginner course and internal resources

What this session is not

  • Not a developer workshop or code-generation tutorial
  • Not a replacement for M365 Copilot — it is the next step
  • Not a deep dive on MCP internals, agent frameworks, or prompt engineering
  • Not a product pitch — it is a hands-on operational introduction
Why this session matters & what it leads to

The problem

Partner-facing roles spend significant time on repeatable operational work: Connect prep, opportunity reviews, workshop follow-ups, status updates, partner briefings. This work is multi-source (PMX, MSX, email, Teams, SharePoint), multi-step, and often redone from scratch each time. M365 Copilot helps with individual tasks but does not connect across tools or produce reusable, structured workflows.

What changes after this session

  • Connect prep drops from 30-60 min to under 10 — one conversation pulls PMX, MSX, email, partner team and synthesizes a structured briefing
  • Workshop notes become action plans immediately — not two weeks later when momentum is lost
  • Workflows become repeatable and shareable — the partner briefing pattern works for any partner, any team member, any week
  • Context stops getting lost — sessions persist across days, decisions and assumptions survive

Impact at scale

  • If 10 PSAs each save 2 hours/week on partner prep, that is 80+ hours/month reinvested in partner-facing work
  • Standardized workflows mean consistent quality across the team — not dependent on one person's approach
  • The skills and patterns built here feed directly into Agency adoption and team-level agentic workflows

Where it leads

  • Short-term: Individual adoption — each attendee has a working setup and tested patterns
  • Medium-term: Team standardization — shared instructions, skills, and agents for common partner workflows
  • Long-term: Organizational capability — Agency-powered, governed, telemetry-tracked agentic workflows across the partner motion

First, reasoning — AI can plan complex, multi-step tasks. Not just autocomplete. It decomposes a problem, figures out the sequence, and executes.

Second, tool use — through MCP, the Model Context Protocol, AI connects to your actual systems. PMX, MSX, your email, your calendar. Real data, real actions.

The result? The barrier between describing what you want and getting it done just collapsed. That is why CLI exists.

60 min 🟢 CALM · 3 min · Land the four properties

SAY: Four properties that make CLI different from M365 Copilot."

Tool-connected — it calls your actual systems through MCP. PMX, MSX, email, calendar, GitHub. Real data.

Repeatable — the same workflow works for any partner. Build the pattern once, reuse it, share it with your team.

Inspectable — the output is an artifact. A structured document, a plan, a file. Not a chat answer that disappears when you close the tab.

Persistent — /resume picks up where you left off. Across days, across sessions. For work that evolves over time, this is the game changer.

2

🎤 TALKING POINT:

Act 3: Generate the structured plan (4 min)

3

💬 Prompt:

2. Phased roadmap — Phase 1 (MVP, 3 months) and Phase 2 (full, 6 months)
3. For each phase: deliverables, Azure services needed, effort estimate, key assumptions
4. Task breakdown — concrete tasks with suggested owners (PSA, PTS, Partner)
5. Open questions — things we need to resolve before Phase 1 starts
6. Risks with mitigations
7. Next steps — what happens this week

Make it actionable enough that someone who wasn't in the workshop could pick this up and run with it."

🎤 TALKING POINT (while it generates):

This is the bridge. The raw notes become a plan. Phased. With owners. With risks. With open questions. You could send this to the partner, share it with your team, or put it in PMX.
