Your Operational Copilot

From M365 help to repeatable, tool-connected partner workflows. Everything you need for the session is here.

Why a CLI when I already have M365 Copilot?

Short answer: different beast. You'll want both.

M365 Copilot is getting genuinely good. It writes Word docs, drafts emails, summarizes Teams meetings, builds slide decks from documents you point it at. Inside Microsoft's apps, it does real work. Lean on it.

Copilot CLI is something else. It runs on your computer in a small text window called a terminal, and from there it can reach into anything you can reach. Your files. Your inbox. PMX. A public website. The tools you use every day. And it can build new things across all of them, in a single prompt.

It has hands. M365 Copilot has eyes.

Where the line really sits:

  • M365 Copilot edits the Word doc that's already open. The CLI builds you a fresh presentation from scratch, pulling numbers from PMX, context from your inbox, and a market view from a public report, all in one go.
  • M365 Copilot summarizes your meetings. The CLI takes a folder of meeting notes and gives you back a partner briefing, a follow-up plan, and a ready-to-send draft email.
  • M365 Copilot answers questions about your documents. The CLI builds a working dashboard or a small prototype just from your description, so you can show the idea instead of explaining it.

If you can express what you want in plain language, the CLI can usually build it. That's the shift. You stop asking "can I do this in Copilot?" and start asking "what should I make next?"

Taste test

"Find my last three meetings with Contoso, summarize what we discussed, check their PMX project status, then draft a follow-up email for me to review before sending."

Calendar + PMX + Outlook + reasoning, chained in one prompt. M365 Copilot can't reach across all four. The CLI can.
