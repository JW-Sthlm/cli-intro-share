Your Operational Copilot

From M365 help to repeatable, tool-connected partner workflows. Everything you need for the session is here.

Why a CLI when I already have M365 Copilot?

Short answer: different beast. You'll want both.

M365 Copilot is getting genuinely good. It writes Word docs, drafts emails, summarizes Teams meetings, builds slide decks from documents you point it at. Inside Microsoft's apps, it does real work. Lean on it.

Copilot CLI is something else. An operator on demand. Less of a tool. More of a colleague. You hand it a task in plain language. It writes its own plan, picks the tools, runs through the work, checks itself, fixes what is broken, and comes back with something new. Twenty minutes later. Sometimes overnight.

M365 Copilot answers questions. The CLI does the work.

What that actually looks like:

  • The thousand-opportunity match. Hand it 5,000 open MSX opportunities in EMEA and your partner list. It scores every opportunity against partner specializations, ACR history, accepted referrals, and segment fit. Comes back with a ranked match list and the reasoning per row. The kind of work a strategy team takes a week on, done in an hour.
  • The deck from scratch. "Build me a 12-slide partner readiness deck for Odaseva on Fabric." It reads PMX history, your recent meeting notes, public Odaseva content, and your own slide library. Comes back with the deck, speaker notes, and a list of what it couldn't verify so you know where to fact-check.
  • The diagnostic. "We're losing momentum on Azure migrations in France. Why?" It looks across PMX projects, related opportunities, recent partner activity, and referral patterns. Comes back with a written analysis pointing to where the friction is and three things you could try.
  • The dashboard you describe out loud. "Show my top 50 EMEA partners by influenced ACR against specializations and open pipeline, interactive, colour-coded by segment." Ten minutes later there's a working HTML page open in your browser.

If you can describe what you want in plain language, the CLI can usually build it. That's the shift. Brief it. Walk away. Come back to work done.
