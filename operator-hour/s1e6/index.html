The sidekick was an agent. Now it's yours.

For six weeks Otto answered your questions in the cohort chat. In the finale we opened the hood, showed exactly how Otto is built, then forked it live into a second agent with its own name and its own job. The repo is public now. Season 2 runs on whoever picks it up.

Recap

Every @mention this season hit a bot called Otto. No mystery about who built it. I did, with the same Copilot tools you have used for six weeks. So for the last episode we stopped talking about Otto and took it apart on screen.

Otto is three things, and none of them are scary. A persona, written in plain text. A pile of knowledge, also plain text, the recap pages and transcripts you already watched. And a publish step that drops it into Teams. That is the whole animal. No model training, nothing exotic underneath.

We built a second agent in front of the room. Cloned Otto's repo, rewrote the persona, and Remy was born: an onboarding sidekick for a made-up partner, Northwind. Out went Otto's course files, in went onboarding material, an FAQ, a first-week checklist. Same shell, different brain. One plain ask to deploy, a few minutes of waiting, and Remy was a live agent you could drop into a Teams chat.

The line that stuck The agent only knows what you feed it. The persona is the voice. The knowledge files are the brain. Change the files and you have changed the agent.

No scary commands. The whole build ran on plain-English asks to the CLI. And if a terminal is not your thing, Copilot Studio and the M365 Agent Builder do the same job with buttons. Same idea, different door in.

You own what it says. The agent answers from your files and nothing else, so the content is on you. Fact-check it. Keep it current. Add a line that says a bot wrote this and to double-check, a reminder for the reader and for you.

What the room asked: do you need a template to start? (No. Otto was built from scratch out of plain text files. There is a starter kit if you want a head start, but a folder of text and one prompt is enough.) What if the deploy throws an error? (It happens. Read the message, hand it back to the agent, go again. That is the loop, not a failure.)

The operator move: pick the thing your team keeps re-explaining and point an agent at it. The onboarding doc nobody finishes, the partner FAQ, the questions you answer twice a week. That folder is your agent's brain. Fork the repo, swap the files, give it a name.

Take-home this week

Fork the repo and stand up your own agent

Take one body of knowledge your team keeps repeating: an onboarding guide, a partner FAQ, last quarter's playbooks. Fork Otto's repo, rewrite the persona, drop your files in, deploy. The Finale Kit has every prompt in order and in plain language. Block an afternoon.

Curious rather than committed? The repo is public. Open it, read it, break it. That counts too.
