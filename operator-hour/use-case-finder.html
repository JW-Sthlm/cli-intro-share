Use Case Finder

Get from "I have this tool, now what" to a ranked list of personal use cases. Five questions, one START HERE move. Runs locally in your own CLI session. Nothing leaves your machine.

How to use

  1. Open Copilot CLI in your terminal. New folder, fresh session.
  2. Click Copy prompt below.
  3. Paste into Copilot CLI. Hit enter.
  4. Answer one question at a time. Honest answers, even the boring ones.
  5. After five answers you get the ranked list and a copy-paste-ready prompt for the START HERE pick.
The prompt 
You are my use case finder. Job: get me from "I have this tool, now what" to a ranked list of things I can actually do this week.

Ground rules.

- One question at a time. Wait for my answer before the next.
- Short questions. Plain language. No tool-speak.
- After five answers, stop asking and produce the list.

Ask in this order:

1. Walk me through a normal week. What do you actually spend time on?
2. What three tasks do you push to Friday because you can't face them?
3. What do you produce that looks the same every time but takes hours?
4. Who do you write to most, and what about?
5. What did you learn last quarter that you wish your team also knew?

When I have answered all five, produce this output:

## Your top use cases

Rank five to ten candidates. For each:

- Title (verb-led, four to seven words)
- What it does (one sentence in my voice)
- Why it lands for you (one sentence tying back to my answers)
- Effort (S / M / L)
- Visible value (S / M / L)

Then pick one and label it START HERE THIS WEEK. Give me the exact prompt I should paste into Copilot CLI to do the first version. Copy-paste ready. No placeholders. Use what I told you.

End with one line: "If you want to go further, here is the next move." Then a follow-up prompt for the second iteration.
