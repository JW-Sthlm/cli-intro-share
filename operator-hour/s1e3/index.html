Reclaim your morning.

The first hour of your day, handed back to you. We point the agent at a real morning: inbox, calendar, and the channel that actually matters. You leave with a brief you can run with one command tomorrow, and a clear read on where Microsoft is taking this next.

Recap

Everyone has a morning routine. The hype version is five a.m., yoga, a cold plunge, something green in a glass. The real one is you crawl to your inbox and it hits you like Mike Tyson. Forty unread before you've found the coffee. That's the routine we fixed. Not inbox-zero theatre, but a routine that reads what landed overnight, tells you what actually needs you, and drafts the rest.

Rules sort. Routines decide. An Outlook rule drops mail in a folder you never open, and it's still on you to go look. A routine reads the inbox, works out what needs you, and leaves the noise filed. One job, three levels: Cowork inside the Copilot you already have, the CLI you build yourself, and Scout on the roadmap. We built level two live.

The live part. A real inbox, sanitised, seeded with the kind of mail that actually lands overnight. One plain sentence, "Sort out my inbox", and the folders filled in front of the room. A packed inbox down to a handful. The trust beat is what stayed behind: mail a real person addressed to me, untouched. When the routine isn't sure, it leaves it right where I'll see it.

The payoff The sort runs on a schedule, and the brief is ready by eight, before I'm even up. I don't start the day in my inbox anymore. I wake up and it's already sorted, the brief's waiting, and the first thing I touch is the actual work.

Why it's safe. Three dumb rules you can read in one breath. Addressed to you stays put. Noise gets its own room. When unsure, keep it. No clever AI guessing what matters to you. Then the real reward, Prompt B: a short morning brief. What changed overnight, the one meeting that needs prep, what's waiting on a reply from you, the three things to do first. Easy replies drafted, nothing sent.

The operator move: pick the one inbox, calendar or channel that owns your first hour, and set the routine on it. Run Prompt A, then Prompt B. Want it hands-off? Grab the give-away script and set the scheduler block once. One honest note from the room: the brief shows up in Copilot, not as an email in your inbox. That's the bit to know before you promise yourself a magic 8am email.
