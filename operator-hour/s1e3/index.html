Reclaim your morning.

The first hour of your day, handed back to you. We point the agent at a real morning: inbox, calendar, and the channel that actually matters. You leave with a brief you can run with one command tomorrow, and a clear read on where Microsoft is taking this next.

What we'll cover
  1. Read the last 24 hours Point the agent at a sanitised inbox, your calendar, and the one channel that matters. It reads everything that landed overnight so you don't have to scroll.
  2. Triage, not just sort What needs you, what needs a reply you can approve in one line, what is noise. The agent proposes. You stay in the loop.
  3. The morning brief One short read: what changed, what is on your calendar, what is waiting on you, what to do first. The thing you wish landed at 08:00.
  4. Make it a routine Save the flow as a skill. Tomorrow it is one command, not a fresh prompt. The routine is the point, not the one-off.
  5. Guardrails What stays on your machine. What never auto-sends. How to keep client detail out of anything that leaves your laptop.
The shift Inbox rules sort mail into folders you never open. A morning routine reads your inbox, calendar and channels, decides what actually needs you, and drafts the rest. You get a three-line brief instead of a backlog.
