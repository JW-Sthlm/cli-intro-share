Level 1 · no terminal needed

Do it in Cowork start here

Cowork is the agent inside Microsoft 365 Copilot. You stay in Outlook, ask in plain language, and let it do the morning read. Replace nothing. These work as written. Review what it proposes before it acts.

How to use

  1. Open Cowork in Microsoft 365 Copilot (the left rail, next to Researcher and Analyst).
  2. Click Copy prompt on Prompt A.
  3. Paste, run, and watch it sort. It shows you what moved and what it kept.
  4. Run Prompt B for your morning brief: what changed, what is on today, what needs you.
  5. Tomorrow, run them again. The routine is the point, not the one-off.
Prompt A · Organize my inbox 
Organize my inbox.

First, show me a quick summary: how many emails arrived, and which ones look like they actually need me.

Then sort the rest into folders, creating them if they don't exist:
- Notifications - automated system mail: GitHub, Azure alerts, approvals, "action required", no-reply senders.
- Newsletters - digests, dev blogs, community roundups, anything I subscribed to.
- Mass - broadcasts and events: town halls, all-hands, community calls, FY kickoffs, office hours, anything sent to a big list.
- Group Invites - meeting invites blasted to a large list where I'm not personally on the To line.

Keep these in my Inbox no matter what:
- Anything where a real person has me on the To or CC line and the group is small (15 people or fewer).
- Anyone I've emailed recently.
- Anything you're unsure about. When in doubt, leave it in my Inbox.

Show me what you moved and what you kept before you finish.
Prompt B · My morning brief 
Give me my morning brief.

Look at the last 24 hours of email, my calendar for today, and anything waiting on me.

Tell me, in a short read:
- What changed overnight that I should know about.
- What's on my calendar today, and which meeting needs prep.
- What's waiting on a reply from me, most important first.
- The three things to do first.

For anything that needs a simple reply, draft it for me to approve. Don't send anything without me.
Why two prompts? Prompt A clears the noise. Prompt B reads what is left and tells you where to start. Run A, then B. That is the whole morning in two pastes.
Level 2 · runs on its own

Take the script the gift

This is the exact script behind the live demo. It sorts your Outlook inbox on your own machine, no AI and no cloud, so it can run quietly every hour without you. You do not need it to get value from Level 1. It is here for the people who want the sorting to just happen.

Notifications Newsletters Mass Group Invites

What it does

  1. Reads every email in your Outlook inbox.
  2. Moves the noise into four folders. Notifications for system mail, Newsletters for digests, Mass for broadcasts and events, Group Invites for large-list meeting invites.
  3. Keeps anything a real person sent to a small group with you on the line. When in doubt, it leaves it in your Inbox.
  4. Prints a short report: what it moved, and what is still waiting for you.
⬇ Download inbox-triage.ps1

Change one line near the top to your own address, then run it:

$MyEmail = 'you@yourcompany.com' pwsh -ExecutionPolicy Bypass -File .\inbox-triage.ps1
To make it automatic: the bottom of the file has a ready-made Windows Task Scheduler block. Paste it once and the sort runs hourly, 08:00 to 17:00, as long as Outlook is open. Nothing leaves your machine.
Level 3 · where this heads

Keep an eye on Scout roadmap

Scout is the first agent in Microsoft's Autopilot family. The difference: it is proactive. Its own identity, working while you are in a meeting or away from the desk, surfacing what needs you instead of waiting to be asked. This is from what Microsoft showed at Build. It is new, it needs your admin to switch it on, and it is not yet part of any daily routine here. Worth knowing it is coming.
