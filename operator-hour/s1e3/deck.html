Where the script came from

I didn't write it. I asked for it.

I opened Copilot CLI and described the job in plain language. It wrote the script. No syntax to learn, no Stack Overflow tabs. If you can describe how you want your inbox sorted, you can build this.

  • 2Tell it the rules in plain words.
  • 3Let it write and test the script.
  • 4Save it. Tomorrow it's one command.

S1E3 · The engine
07
What it actually does

Three rules. That's the engine.

No clever AI deciding what matters to you. Three plain rules you can read in one breath. Dumb on purpose, so it never hides a real email.

  • 1Addressed to you stays put. Anything sent directly to you stays in the inbox. Always.
  • 2Noise gets its own room. Newsletters, notifications and broadcasts move to their own folders.
  • 3When unsure, keep it. If it can't tell, it leaves it in the inbox. It never guesses a real one into a folder.
Slide 7 / 12
←→
DO: This is the trust slide. Non-technical room needs to believe it won't lose mail. Lean on rule 3. Read all three slowly. 75 seconds. SAY: People worry an agent will hide something important. So the rules are deliberately simple. If a message is addressed to you, it stays in your inbox, full stop. The newsletters and the system noise get their own folders. And when it isn't sure, it does the safe thing and leaves it in front of you. You can read the whole logic in one breath. That's the point.
