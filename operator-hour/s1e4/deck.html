S1E4 · The recipe
04
The recipe

Problem in. Prototype out.

Five steps, the same every time. You bring the customer's problem and their words. Copilot does the typing. You stay the one deciding.

  • 1Frame the problem. The one moment the prototype has to prove. Not the platform.
  • 2Feed it substance. Their meeting notes, their data shape, their words. The part a generic demo can't fake.
  • 3Build something clickable. Plain language in, one HTML file out. No installs.
  • 4Show it. Let them click. Demo it the way you'd show a customer.
  • 5Hand it over clean. README plus infrastructure-as-code, so an engineer takes it from there.

S1E4 · Keep it honest
10
Demo-grade, and say so

Three rules keep it honest.

A prototype earns trust by being clear about what it is. Three rules, no exceptions.

  • 1Fictional data only. The customer's shape, never the customer's records. Invent the rows.
  • 2Say it's a prototype. On the screen, in the footer, and out loud in the room.
  • 3Engineers take it to production. It gets there through a repo and a review. Never straight from your demo laptop.

SAY: So before you all run off and build something, just three quick rules. One, fictional data, always. You want their data shape and their vocabulary, you know, but never their actual records. So invent the rows, keep the structure. Two, label it. The footer on my kiosk literally says prototype, and I say it out loud in every single demo. Nobody should find out later that the thing they were clicking was a mock. And three, it reaches production through engineers, through a repo and a review. Because our job here is to start the conversation, not to kind of sneak something into someone's estate.
