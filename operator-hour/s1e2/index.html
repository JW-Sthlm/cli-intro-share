Decks that win the room.

Six moves from messy notes to a deck that lands. Restate, research, model the room, outline, build, polish. I bring a real-feeling brief — fake partner, real shape — and we ship it live. The deck is the receipt. The thinking is the win.

What we'll cover
  1. Restate the brief The agent reads the mess better than the mess reads itself. Notes go in. A sharp sentence comes out — what does the room actually need to believe by the end of the hour?
  2. Research with real tools Public web, internal MCPs, partner news. The part M365 Copilot in PowerPoint can't reach. Tonight: Work Trend Index 2026 + public Frontier transformation case studies.
  3. Model the room Five people, five wants. CFO needs a number. CEO needs a story. Head of People needs a talent answer. Find the through-line that lands for all of them.
  4. Outline + critique Six slides, one argument each, no bullet lists. Then the push-back: "What's the weakest slide?" Adjust before you build.
  5. Build the deck Frontier-style HTML. Open in browser. Presenter view in a second window. Five minutes from outline to artifact.
  6. Polish surgically Two real fixes against the deck in front of you. Reload. Ship. Polish before the deck exists is just guessing.
The shift M365 Copilot in PowerPoint gives you slides. CLI gives you a thinking partner that ships a thing. And the thing doesn't have to be a deck — same rhythm builds briefing docs, dashboards, prototypes, automations. We'll show that too.
